What is Kiwisaver?

Kiwisaver is a government organised savings programme which helps you to save up for your retirement. This savings are made up through a regular contribution from your salary. You get to choose from the available Kiwisaver schemes. There could be a little contribution from your employer too. There is also a contribution that happens annually from the government itself.


How does it work?

A percentage of your pay is deducted at a rate of 3%, 4% or 8% and placed into your Kiwisaver account. This is done automatically so that you do not 'miss' the money

You get the money when you're 65 or when:

  • Buying your first home
  • Moving overseas permanently
  • Suffering financial hardship
  • Seriously ill

The Benefits

Kiwisaver is an easy way of saving for your future. You don't miss the money like you do with a normal savings account as it never enters your bank account. Your Kiwisaver account will also receive yearly contributions from the government at a maximum of $521.43. This is known as annual member tax credit.
